Traditional Algerian Couscous

A beloved North African dish, simple yet deeply flavorful

Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 45 minutes
Servings: 6 people

About This Dish

Couscous is the national dish of Algeria, traditionally served on Fridays and special occasions. This hearty meal combines fluffy semolina granules with a rich vegetable stew, creating a perfect balance of flavors and textures. The beauty of couscous lies in its simplicity and the warmth it brings to family gatherings.

Ingredients

For the Couscous:

  • 500g medium-grain couscous
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 500ml warm water

For the Stew:

  • 500g lamb or chicken, cut into pieces
  • 3 carrots, peeled and halved
  • 2 zucchini, halved
  • 2 turnips, peeled and quartered
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained
  • 3 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro and parsley
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil

Step-by-Step Instructions

Place the couscous in a large bowl. Add salt and drizzle with olive oil, mixing with your fingers to coat the grains. Gradually add warm water, stirring gently. Let it rest for 10 minutes to absorb the liquid. Fluff with a fork to separate the grains, then steam in a couscousière or steamer basket for 20 minutes until light and fluffy.

Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic, cumin, and paprika, stirring for another minute until fragrant. Add the meat pieces and brown them on all sides.

Stir in the tomato paste, then add all the vegetables: carrots, turnips, zucchini, and tomatoes. Add the chickpeas and enough water to cover everything. Season with salt and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30-35 minutes until the vegetables are tender and the meat is cooked through.

Mound the fluffy couscous on a large serving platter. Arrange the meat and vegetables on top, then ladle some of the flavorful broth over everything. Garnish generously with fresh cilantro and parsley. Serve hot with extra broth on the side and enjoy this comforting Algerian classic with family and friends.

Chef's Tips

For the most authentic flavor, use a traditional couscousière (a special two-tiered pot) to steam the couscous over the simmering stew. The couscous absorbs the aromatic steam, making it even more flavorful. If you don't have one, a regular steamer works perfectly well.

Traditional Algerian couscous is often served with a spicy harissa sauce on the side for those who enjoy extra heat. You can also add raisins and caramelized onions for a sweet and savory variation.

Enjoy this taste of Algeria! Share this recipe with loved ones and keep the tradition alive.
